The Winter Shedding: Unraveling Hair Loss in Women

November 22, 2025 at 12:49 PM

As the colder months set in, many women notice an increase in hair shedding. Understanding the reasons behind this phenomenon can help alleviate concerns and promote healthier hair care practices.

Seasonal Changes

During winter, the drop in temperature and humidity levels can lead to dry air, which causes hair to become brittle and more prone to breakage. Additionally, the scalp can lose moisture, leading to dryness that may result in increased shedding.

Hormonal Fluctuations

Winter can also bring about changes in hormonal levels due to variations in daylight. Reduced exposure to sunlight may decrease vitamin D production, which is essential for hair health, potentially leading to increased hair loss in women.

Stress and Lifestyle Factors

The winter season can introduce stressors such as holiday pressures and changes in routine, which may contribute to hair shedding. Stress can trigger a condition known as telogen effluvium, where hair follicles prematurely enter a resting phase, leading to increased hair loss.

Stay informed and proactive about your hair care this winter to maintain luscious locks all season long!
